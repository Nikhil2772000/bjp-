About

As you know, Maharashtra Vidhan-sabha elections are just around the corner and like always there are many names doing the rounds as a potential candidate to represent our constituency. I am humbled as well as excited to be considered as one of the potential candidates, based on the feedback received from people. Hence I feel it's my moral responsibility to share some more details about myself with all of you to know me better.

As usual like any other women I too having two names; my maiden name being Ms. Sulakshana Ashok Shilwant and after marriage I am known as Mrs Sulakshana Raju Dhar. Having said that I am a daughter, a sister, a wife, a mother and with your blessings presently also I am corporator of ward No 20. I was born in my hometown 'Thomse' a small rural Village in Patan Taluka of Satara District where I completed my primary education. Owing to my father's official transfer I completed my secondary education from a municipal school in Pimpri and Junior college from S.N.D.T women's college in Pune. I finally completed my Graduation (B.com) from the renowned Symbiosis College in Pune. Along with that, I also received a Doctorate of Social Science from The American University, USA in 2021. I have experienced a very diversified group of friends and colleagues from across religion, color, caste and financial status. Looking back today I feel this massive transition and exposure has helped meto understand the difficulties and requirements of both rural as well as urban citizens better.

My inclination towards Social Services and Politics has been inherited from my family. My great grandfather was an active participant in the Patri Sarkarof the Kranti Singh Nana Patil. My grandfather was a school teacher in Satara Zilla Parishad School. My grandmother was not just a homemaker but she has also served as Sarpanch of our Village for 5-year term winning the election unopposed. My uncle is a Director of Patan Taluka Sarkari milk Union from past 25 years, my mother is a successful homemaker and two brothers are successfully handling their own business. I had an inter-caste marriage with a Kashmiri Pandit Mr Raju Dhar with blessings of our families. My husband is running a successful Pharmaceuticals business of his own.

My late father, Dr. Ashok Shilwant, was employed in a government job. Through the Ashok Sarvangeen Vikas Society, he worked for 31 years in various fields such as social work, education, culture, literature, art, and cooperation. Due to this, he was known not only throughout Maharashtra but also in India as true disciple and follower of 'Samrat Ashoka'. To elevate the economic status of the underprivileged in society, he established the Ashok Nagari Cooperative Bank. I have been managing the responsibilities at this bank, starting from the position of Junior Clerk to now serving as the Chief Manager. The inspiration for social work that I received from my father, along with my experience in the cooperative sector, has enabled me to directly connect with the public and helped me to build a strong bond with them.

Due to the Indian constitution laid down by Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar it was possible to have reservation for backward class community in elections. Later with the initiative of Shri Sharadchandraji Pawar Saheb there was 50% reservation for women in local body elections. As a result of this I was given an opportunity to contest for Corporation election in PCMC at Sant Tukaram Nagar division 20, and with the support, guidance and blessings from all of you I was elected with huge vote margins in my first attempt in year 2017. Since the beginning I have been working day-night in Municipal Corporation as a representative of ward 20 from the past seven years. I have got the opportunity to work at various platforms in the Municipal Corporation like 'Women & Child Welfare Committee, Law Committee, Education Committee, and City Improvement Committee. Even though I am a part of opposition party in the local body I consider it's my duty to work hard, bring fresh ideas and solutions to make strategic decision without opposition from the government to up lift our society and fulfil basic needs of mankind.

With everyone's blessings, in 2019, I was announced as the candidate for the Pimpri Assembly constituency by the Nationalist Congress Party. However, due to some unexpected local political circumstances, I missed that opportunity. Despite this, I did not lose heart. I remained loyal and faithfully worked for the candidates chosen by the party, following the orders given by the party. Those candidates were also elected. A year ago, after the party split, I was the only councillor who remained true to my beliefs, principles, and the will of my beloved voters, and I steadfastly remained loyal to the esteemed Sharad Pawar Saheb.

Over the past seven years, I have experienced many ups and downs. I lost my beloved son, who was a divine gift of motherhood to me. My father, who shaped me inside and out, left us. Recently, my father-in-law, who cared for me like a father, also passed away. I was able to recover and support my family through these numerous challenges because all of you truly became my support and gave strength to me and my family.

'Your life should not just be like waves that crash against the shore and then retreat. You need to have the strength and patience like the ocean, which faces hundreds of storms at once and emerges victorious!'

In my childhood along with my friends I was an active Sangh Shakha member, I was involved in NSS camps in my college and actively participated in every task. I have attended Vipassana (meditation) as well, so as to discipline my body and mind, I believe it has become my way of living life now.

I feel it's very crucial for every citizen to know in-depth details about their local candidates like their personal, educational and skills related background; to choose among the various alternatives available. Hence instead of putting a hoarding, roadside banner feel communicating via this letter will be appropriate. This is my little effort to reach out to each and every one of you for...!
