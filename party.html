A Legacy of Service — The Congress Party’s Guiding Philosophy

With warm regards,

Respected Sharad Pawar Saheb has always placed the common people, the marginalized, the economically and socially disadvantaged, tribals, farmers, women, workers, and youth at the center of his politics. Consequently, during his extensive tenure as Chief Minister and as a Central Minister, he has made numerous decisions with the welfare of these groups in mind. Over a remarkable journey of 57 years in social and political arenas, Sharad Pawar Saheb has faced many challenges. Yet, while preserving the legacy and ideals of the people's leader, the late Yashwantrao Chavan Saheb, he has continued to make significant contributions to both the country and Maharashtra, firmly believing that no struggle is too great when it comes to the welfare of the people.

Currently, political morality in the country has deteriorated due to certain specific elements. The ruling government has created disillusionment among ordinary citizens under the pretense of promoting development. Inflation has risen sharply, farmers who feed the nation are themselves starving, law and order concerns have intensified, unemployment continues to grow, and challenges in education and healthcare for the underprivileged are worsening. Furthermore, in the name of caste and religion, the ruling party is inciting the youth and creating divisions among communities for political gain.

The government is misusing state machinery to suppress voices that oppose it, attempting to stifle democratic dissent. While the hardships of the underprivileged deepen, the ruling party remains callously preoccupied with breaking opposition parties and weakening social organizations. At such a time, it is essential for the people to show the rulers their rightful place. I firmly believe that in the coming period, this anti-Maharashtra government will indeed be shown its place by the self-respecting Tutaras of the Nationalist Congress Party and by the power of the common people.

We will fight for the rights of ordinary people and for their overall development... We will win! Thank you.

Sharad Pawar Saheb's Notable Contributions to Pimpri-Chinchwad City

The city of Pimpri-Chinchwad emerged from the vision of the late Yashwantrao Chavan, late Dhananjayrao Gadgil, late Annasaheb Magar, and late Ramkrishna More. In the subsequent years, Sharad Pawar Saheb has played the most significant role in the city's development. His influence spans politics, social work, trade, industry, and labor.

Sharad Pawar Saheb’s name is prominent in every sector—politics, social work, trade, industry, and labor. Every time a new bridge, hospital, school, building, park, or swimming pool is inaugurated in the city, Sharadchandra Pawar’s name is invariably mentioned on the foundation and inauguration plaques.

The Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) is relatively recent. Before its formation, during the periods of the Socialist Congress and Congress parties, Pimpri-Chinchwad city and the surrounding villages received strong support from the revered Pawar Saheb—from village heads to every member of the gram panchayats.

Hundreds of respected names can be listed, such as late Madhukar Anna Pawle, late Fakirbhai Pansare, late Sadaba Kate, late Hiramman Barane, late Pandurang Kalate, late Bhiku Waghere Patil, late Vithoba Lande Patil, late MLA Lakshman Jagtap, late Rangnath Kudale, late Digambar Kalbhor, late Rangnath Fugge, late Sopanrao Bhoir, and many more. For all of them, the direction provided by Pawar Saheb was always considered the right path.

In 1989, a strike by Tata Motors workers severely affected the company's operations. The strike was successfully resolved by Sharad Pawar Saheb; otherwise, Tata was preparing to shut down operations here. In 1992–93, 188 acres of land were allocated for Tata’s expansion project (Mitsubishi-Benz), attracting significant investment and generating large-scale employment.

In 1994, Maharashtra was selected to host the National Games. Since Rajiv Gandhi had changed the format to resemble the Olympic Games in 1985, this was only the third national event. As Maharashtra's Chief Minister, Sharad Pawar Saheb ensured the successful organization of these sports competitions, setting a benchmark across the country. This led to the establishment of the Shiv Chhatrapati International Sports Complex in Balewadi.

Sharad Pawar Saheb played a crucial role in the construction of the Bhakti Shakti Memorial in Nigdi, commemorating the historic meeting between Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and Jagadguru Sant Tukaram Maharaj. Under the leadership of farmer leader Sharad Joshi, the farmers’ agitation was also resolved smoothly within four days, thanks to Sharad Pawar Saheb’s intervention.

The struggle of Bajaj Auto workers is a historic moment in the city’s labor movement. Its resolution was made possible through Pawar Saheb's leadership. The bond between industrialist Rahul Bajaj and Sharad Pawar remains strong to this day. For over twenty years, the Hindustan Antibiotics (HA) company has faced significant challenges, and regardless of which union represented the workers, Sharad Pawar Saheb consistently ensured central government support to address their issues.

Due to Sharad Pawar Saheb’s foresight, the Rajiv Gandhi IT Park was established in the Hinjewadi area. This transformative decision fueled the comprehensive development of Pimpri-Chinchwad and Pune districts. Today, the IT park provides employment to nearly five lakh youth and has greatly strengthened the region’s economic landscape.

Whether it was the JNNURM (Jawaharlal Nehru National Urban Renewal Mission) or the Smart City initiative during the UPA government, Sharad Pawar Saheb consistently ensured that Pimpri-Chinchwad received priority. Initially, JNNURM included only Pune city, but due to Pawar Saheb’s efforts, Pimpri-Chinchwad was added, resulting in a development allocation of ₹3,500 crores.

In 2014, when Narendra Modi from the BJP became Prime Minister, only Pune was included in the Smart City project. However, Sharad Pawar Saheb ensured that Pimpri-Chinchwad was recognized as an independent city and included in the scheme, enabling it to benefit from numerous major and minor development projects. Today, development across every corner of the city reflects his lasting influence.

For patients coming from Pune district and across Maharashtra, Sharad Pawar Saheb played a key role in establishing the Yashwantrao Chavan Memorial (YCM) Hospital in Pimpri. Due to his contribution, YCM Hospital has become a leading healthcare institution offering excellent patient care.

Sharad Pawar Saheb has consistently focused on well-planned roads, infrastructure, education, healthcare, and employment in Pimpri-Chinchwad, laying the foundation for a true model Smart City.
